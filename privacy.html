Karto. — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

What this covers

Karto is a personal vocabulary-training app. This page explains what data it collects, why, and what control you have over it. It's written in plain language rather than dense legal text, but it's meant to accurately describe how the app actually works.

Who runs Karto

Karto is an independently run personal project, not a company. If you have a question or a request about your data, you can reach the person running it via the contact noted at the bottom of this page.

What data is collected

  • Account info: your email address, and if you sign in with Google, your name and profile email as provided by Google. If you sign up with email/password instead, only your email and an encrypted password are stored.
  • Your vocabulary data: the words, translations, notes, and practice progress (which box each word is in, next review date) that you add while using the app.
  • Nothing else. Karto does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking technologies, and does not sell or share your data with advertisers.

Why this data is processed

Purely to run the service you're using: to identify your account, keep your word list separate from everyone else's, and let your progress follow you across devices. This is processed on the legal basis of performing the service you signed up for, not on the basis of tracking or marketing consent.

Who else touches your data

Two service providers process data on Karto's behalf, strictly to make the app function:

  • Supabase — hosts the database and handles authentication. See Supabase's Privacy Policy.
  • Google — if you choose "Sign in with Google," Google handles that authentication step. See Google's Privacy Policy.

Neither is used for advertising or analytics purposes within Karto — only to run login and storage.

How your data is protected

Your vocabulary data is protected by database-level access rules (row-level security), meaning even at the infrastructure level, only your own logged-in account can read or write your words — not other users, and not accessible by simply knowing the app's public configuration.

Your rights

You can, at any time:

  • See your data — everything you've stored is visible directly in the app's Words tab.
  • Delete individual words — using the delete option next to any word.
  • Delete your entire account and all data — using the "Delete my account" option in the app, or by contacting the address below if you'd prefer it done manually.
  • Ask questions about what's stored or how — via the contact below.

Cookies

Karto does not use cookies for tracking or advertising. It stores a login session so you stay signed in, which is considered strictly necessary for the service and doesn't require a cookie consent banner under EU rules.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes meaningfully, the "last updated" date above will reflect it.

Contact

For any privacy question or data request, reach out via the contact information listed on the Karto GitHub repository.
